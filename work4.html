Holistic Rubric – Monologue Assessment (Grade 9)

A (Excellent)

• Speaks quite fluently (80–90%), with 3–4 short pauses or self-corrections.

• Uses adequate vocabulary (around B1 level) and a mix of simple and some complex grammar, with 3–5 minor errors.

• Ideas are mostly clear and organized, though sometimes repetitive.

• Pronunciation is generally correct, with few mispronunciations that do not affect understanding.

• Shows good confidence and ability to express thoughts independently.

B (Good)

• Speaks fluently (90–100%) with 1–2 short pauses.

• Uses a wide range of vocabulary (B1–B2 level) and varied grammatical structures, making only 1–2 minor errors.

• Ideas are well-organized, clear, and logically connected.

• Pronunciation and intonation are natural and easy to understand.

• Demonstrates confidence, maintains good eye contact, and communicates ideas effectively.

C (Satisfactory)

• Speech is understandable (70–80%), but includes frequent pauses (5–7) and hesitation.

• Uses basic vocabulary (A2–B1 level) and simple grammar, with 6–8 noticeable errors that sometimes affect meaning.

• Ideas are simple and sometimes unclear, transitions may be weak.

• Pronunciation occasionally interferes with understanding (2–3 clear mistakes).

• Shows some effort to communicate, but needs more fluency and confidence.

D (Needs Improvement)

• Speech is very hesitant and fragmented (below 70%), with many pauses or unfinished sentences.

• Uses limited vocabulary (A1–A2 level) and frequent grammar errors (9+ errors) that make comprehension difficult.

• Ideas are disorganized or incomplete, lacking structure.

• Pronunciation and intonation often make understanding difficult (several repeated mistakes).

• Shows low confidence and struggles to express ideas without teacher support.
